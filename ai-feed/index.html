Apple filed a federal lawsuit in Northern California on July 10 accusing OpenAI of systematically stealing hardware trade secrets through former Apple engineers - notably Tang Tan, who once led iPhone and Apple Watch product design, and Chang Liu, who downloaded dozens of confidential hardware files before joining OpenAI. What's moving

  • Jony Ive is not personally named, but his io Products firm is a defendant; the $6.5 billion acquisition that was supposed to give OpenAI design credibility is now itself a legal liability before a single product has shipped from it

South Korea Commits $880 Billion to AI Chips - the Biggest National Compute Bet in History

South Korea's President Lee Jae Myung unveiled a coordinated $880 billion (1,350 trillion won) investment plan covering semiconductors, AI data centers, and robotics - with Samsung Group and SK Group each building two new chip fabrication plants in the country's southwest. The total is roughly 5% of South Korea's 2024 GDP, more than the entire GDP of Switzerland, and is framed as sovereign infrastructure on par with highways. The primary target is high-bandwidth memory (HBM), the chip category that makes large-scale AI inference economically possible and where Samsung and SK Hynix already hold more than 95% of global supply.

What's moving
  • Power grid availability is the binding constraint: a single AI megacluster requires a quarter of Seoul's total power demand, and grid buildout is running two to three years behind the fab construction schedule - an $880 billion capital commitment gated by electricity, not money or technology
  • The HBM angle is the most strategically significant: AI training and inference economics are dominated by memory bandwidth, and South Korea's near-monopoly on HBM supply gives this investment outsized leverage - whoever controls HBM gates the economics of the entire frontier AI industry
  • The 2034 completion timeline means most new capacity comes online well after current AI chip demand projections are obsolete; the real question is whether Korea is consolidating a dominant position or making a decade-long bet that the compute supercycle has barely started
