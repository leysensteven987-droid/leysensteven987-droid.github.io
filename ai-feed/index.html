Claude Fable 5 Returns After 18-Day Export Control Standoff

The Trump administration lifted its export controls on Anthropic's flagship Fable 5 and Mythos 5 models on June 30, ending an 18-day shutdown triggered by a jailbreak that Amazon researchers found could automate software vulnerability discovery. Anthropic's fix - a single safety filter reviewed by Commerce's own AI standards body CAISI - is the first time a frontier model has been unblocked via a targeted mitigation rather than a full safety overhaul. The case now sets the template for how export control disputes between AI companies and the US government get resolved.

What's moving
  • The original June 12 order required Anthropic to block any foreign national from accessing Fable 5 and Mythos 5 - including its own non-citizen staff - and because real-time nationality verification was not practical, Anthropic shut both models down for all users globally for 18 days
  • The resolution hinged on a single classifier block targeting the exact technique Amazon flagged, with Commerce's Center for AI Standards and Innovation reviewing and signing off - establishing that a targeted filter, not a full model rebuild, can satisfy a US export control requirement
  • Fable 5 went live again on Claude.ai and Claude Code on July 1; Pro, Max, Team, and select Enterprise users get 50% of weekly usage through July 7 before the model transitions to standard usage credits

UN Opens First Global AI Governance Dialogue in Geneva - All 193 Nations at the Table

The United Nations opened its first Global Dialogue on AI Governance today in Geneva - a two-day summit bringing all 193 member states alongside industry, academia, and civil society to negotiate common governance approaches for the first time. Unlike prior AI governance forums dominated by wealthy nations, this dialogue was mandated by the UN General Assembly and runs alongside the WSIS Forum and ITU's AI for Good Summit, making this week the most concentrated multilateral AI policy moment of 2026. The goal is ensuring governance reflects every nation's priorities, not just the most technologically advanced.

What's moving

  • The talks opened against a backdrop of UN warnings of "catastrophic harm" from uncoordinated AI development and the Five Eyes June 23 advisory warning that AI-enabled cyberattacks are "months, not years" away - giving the governance discussions immediate operational urgency beyond abstract risk framing
  • ITU's AI for Good Global Summit runs concurrently through July 10, with industry, government, and civil society sessions covering AI infrastructure, access equity, and safety - the Geneva week concentrates more AI governance activity in one place than any prior event

Meituan Opens LongCat-2.0 - 1.6 Trillion Parameters, Zero NVIDIA Chips

Chinese food-delivery giant Meituan dropped a 1.6-trillion-parameter open-source coding model on June 30 that was trained entirely on domestically produced Chinese chips - no NVIDIA, no Google TPUs. LongCat-2.0 is the first frontier-scale model to complete full pre-training and inference end-to-end on non-Western hardware, directly challenging the assumption that US chip export restrictions have set Chinese frontier AI development back by years. It had already been leading OpenRouter's usage charts under the codename "Owl Alpha" before Meituan revealed its identity.

What's moving
  • Meituan used a 50,000-card cluster of domestic Chinese chips for both pre-training and inference - a scale never previously attempted on Chinese hardware - demonstrating that chip independence is operationally real at frontier scale, not just a stated goal
  • The model uses a Mixture-of-Experts architecture activating 33B-56B of 1.6T parameters per token, with a 1M-token context window, and scored 59.5% on SWE-Bench Pro; its practical inference costs are competitive with leading Western open models
  • LongCat-2.0 is MIT licensed with no usage restrictions - any developer globally can fine-tune, deploy, or build commercial products on it, a direct contrast to the controlled-access strategies at OpenAI and Anthropic at the frontier

Five Eyes: Frontier AI Will Reshape Cyberattacks in Months, Not Years

The Five Eyes intelligence alliance - the US, UK, Canada, Australia, and New Zealand - issued a rare joint public advisory warning that frontier AI models will fundamentally transform offensive cyber capabilities faster than the industry currently expects. "The timeline is not years, it is months," the agencies stated, calling on governments and businesses to act now on defenses. The warning came directly after the US government's emergency export controls on Anthropic's Fable 5 over a jailbreak that could automate software vulnerability discovery.

What's moving
  • Five Eyes identified the core threat vector: AI models that can autonomously identify production system vulnerabilities and generate working exploits - the same capability that triggered the Fable 5 export controls - deployed at scale and speed that current patch cycles were not designed to handle
  • The advisory carries unusual weight as a coordinated intelligence community call to action across five countries rather than a single-nation warning, and is shaping the agenda at the UN AI Governance Dialogue opening in Geneva today
  • Five Eyes also highlighted the defensive upside: organizations deploying AI in their own security operations can detect vulnerabilities earlier, monitor anomalous behavior at machine speed, and respond faster - the same capability that creates the threat can neutralize it if defenders deploy first
