China's $295B AI Compute Grid Locks Out Nvidia and AMD

China's National Development and Reform Commission is finalizing a $295 billion, five-year blueprint to build a nationwide interconnected AI compute grid operated by state firms China Mobile and China Telecom. The plan mandates at least 80% domestic hardware sourcing from Huawei and others, explicitly squeezing out Nvidia and AMD. When power-grid integration is included, total projected investment could reach $740 billion.

What's moving
  • The 80% domestic-chip mandate forces reliance on Huawei's Ascend series at production training scale - a live test of whether China's domestic silicon can match Nvidia's B200 ecosystems on real workloads for the first time
  • The grid model knits scattered data centers into a single state-controlled compute layer by 2028 - architecturally distinct from US hyperscaler buildouts, creating unified national AI infrastructure rather than competing private clouds
  • US export controls designed to slow China's AI progress are accelerating domestic chip investment instead; the $295B plan is the clearest evidence yet that controls are reshaping supply chains rather than containing capability development

MiniMax M3 Hits 1-Million-Token Context at 5-10% of Frontier Cost

Shanghai-based MiniMax released M3 on June 1 - an open-weight multimodal model with a 1-million-token context window powered by a novel MiniMax Sparse Attention mechanism that avoids quadratic compute growth. On SWE-Bench Pro M3 scored 59%, ahead of GPT-5.5 and Gemini 3.1 Pro, at a claimed cost of just 5-10% of comparable proprietary models. Model weights are being published on Hugging Face with a full technical report.

What's moving
  • The Sparse Attention mechanism makes 1M-token context economically viable without exponential compute cost, opening long-context workloads to open-weight production use for the first time and challenging proprietary long-context models directly
  • M3 supports text, image, and video inputs natively and is optimized for long-horizon coding-agent workflows, competing head-on with GPT-5.5 Instant and Claude Opus 4.8 in the enterprise agentic pipeline market
  • Open-weight release on Hugging Face and GitHub gives enterprises and researchers a viable alternative to proprietary long-context models for the first time at this capability level, accelerating commoditization of frontier-grade context windows
